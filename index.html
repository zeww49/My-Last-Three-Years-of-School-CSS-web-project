Introduction

Over the past three years, my school journey has been a meaningful period of growth and self-discovery. Beyond academics, I learned more about who I am, faced challenges that tested my strength, and developed resilience through difficult moments.

Each obstacle became an opportunity to grow, shaping my character, confidence, and determination. These years were not just about finishing school, but about discovering my identity and building the foundation for my future.

Sophomore Year

Sophomore year has been filled with both pleasant and stressful changes. While my personal life became more peaceful amid the chaos surrounding my household, it opened up opportunities to discover new things: managing my daily routine more responsibly, improving my time management skills, and building confidence through self-expression.

Academically, I faced pressure to improve my grades and successfully complete my personal project with a high score, which became one of my biggest accomplishments of the year. Balancing school expectations with personal growth was challenging, especially while trying to figure out what I am truly interested in pursuing after high school. Despite the stress, this year taught me to accept myself, embrace my flaws, and understand that making mistakes are part of learning and becoming stronger.

Junior Year

Although it was impossible to escape the college lectures and conversations from parents and teachers, this year was filled with promises. After facing massive changes in my friend group, I continued to adapt through the changes and accepted the way the transition is.

I was more focused on academics and becoming more involved in extracurriculars to discover what I enjoy the most. I became more satisfied with how much my hard work paid off in school. After I had lost my father, I was left on my own to discover more about understanding pre-calculus and other math topics.

Senior Year

This year has been a mix of fun and hectic moments, filled with excitement for a new chapter while balancing some of my biggest challenges yet. Over the past three years, I pushed myself academically by taking my first challenging online class, which taught me discipline and time management, and later taking another online course while applying to colleges.

Managing essays, deadlines, and schoolwork at the same time wasn’t easy, but it showed me how much I’ve grown. The college search process was both exciting and overwhelming, as I explored different schools and thought about my future. On top of that, self-commuting long distances every day strengthened my independence and responsibility. These experiences shaped me into a more confident, determined, and prepared student ready for what’s next.
